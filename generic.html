Consulting and More

Wake up at 4:30AM every morning. and starts his morning with a jog 4 miles around a lake near his house.

Drive to work around 6:00AM. This is an excellent way to skip traffic, get to work early, and pump out some work while there are no e-mails or pings throughout the day yet.

Get home around 5:00PM. With remaining free time in the day spend time with family. Work on creating content. Edit videos. If there happens to be a lot to complete at work, then make sure to complete all important tasks before taking off for the day.

Finish off the day with stretches and mobility, core, and strength exercises. Use this as an opportunity to also destress. Sleep by 10:00PM, and get ready to repeat for the next day.

4 Mile Jog

A long run in the morning, plus some body weight exercises, is a great way to kick start the day. It enforces discipline, because it forces you to wake up incredibly early everyday. Also, running also incredibly mental, and being able to accomplish a challenging task first thing after waking up will start the day on a good note. Furthermore, running will help clear the mind. I very rarely run without listening to either the news, a podcast, audiobook, or Spotify music. (Most of the time I listen to music towards the end of the run for extra motivation). I also perform 100 push ups, and 40 pull ups before I call it a day.

I do not have my first meal, until typically around 11:00-1:00PM. I try to eat within a fasting window to stay leaner, and also keep my mind from being dulled down by food or coffee. I will drink coffee later in the day if I need a boost, mornings are usually for tea and water. I try to keep most of my meals low carb, with high protein. I find that carbs make it easier for me to gain weight, and I prefer feeling light after each meal. Except on weekends, I tend to be much more open to eat whatever is on my plate, because I need to take a break sometimes.
